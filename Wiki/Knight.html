    • Missions

    The missions can vary from chores, to guard duty, to escorts, investigations, expeditions, explorations, hunting, spying to war missions and many others.

    High School

    Students in the 7th grade to 9th grade are called Pages and in the 10th grade to 12th grade are Squires. Each of the Pages get a kind of mentorship from one of the Squires. The Squires are supposed to teach them and by doing so they learn themselves. They get a team from their class during the 10th grade and they are a part of this team even after graduation because they have teamwork and know each other well. The Squire teams also get mentorship from real knights, one knight per team, and get to come with them on missions, serve them and learn from them during the 3 years. There’s a few weeks during each year where the Squires are supposed to be with the Knight for all of their missions that entire time. At graduation the Squire is dubbed a Knight but sometimes continues the mentorship with the older knights.

    Culture

    Chivalric Code

    Every knight has to act according to the Code of Chivalry which is the following rules:
  • To serve those placed in authority in valour and faith
  • To protect the weak and defenceless
  • To assist and support widows and orphans
  • To never provoke an opponent or attack first
  • To never attack an unarmed or defenceless opponent
  • To act honourably and mercifully
  • To always strive to do the right thing
  • To fight for the welfare of all
  • To serve and assist those placed in authority
  • To carry out any missions given and protect teammates with one's life
  • To guard the honour of fellow knights
  • To abstain unfairness, meanness and deceit
  • To keep faith
  • To always speak the truth
  • To persevere to the end in any enterprise begun
  • To show respect to everyone
  • To always help those in need
  • To never chase rewards or fame
  • To never refuse a challenge from an equal
  • To never turn the back upon a foe

    • Tournaments

    There are often tournaments where knights either from the kingdom or from kingdoms all over the world come to compete in various activities. The World Knight Tournament is where the best knights from around the world compete to test their skills.

    Junior Tournaments

    There's also the Junior Knight Tournament where knight students from different high schools in the kingdom compete against each other and the winner goes to represent their kingdom in the Junior Knight World Tournament against the winners in other kingdoms.

    Death

    A ceremony when a knight dies. They kneel with their sword and hold up a flame.

    Appearance

    Knights are mainly humans and in some cases human hybrids or beings whose appearance is close enough to be passable as humans without anyone noticing they're not. The knights wear armor and are equipped with weapons of their choice.

    History

    At first, only noblemen could become knights, but one talented boy changed that, he wasn’t a nobleman but he was accepted as a knight and after that, you didn’t have to be a nobleman to become one. He became one of the most famous knights in history for his courage and skills. The old king knew he had to trust the knights with his life, and therefore only noblemen could serve as knights. The boy was rejected, but he was very chivalrous and earned his place.
